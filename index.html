Returning

After the one good poem
came the many bad ones.

It was time to return home,
back to the old ways of living.

I lay on the terminal floor,
raincoat crushed beneath my head,

awaiting departure.
By dawn I would be on my way,

asleep I hoped against the window
only dreaming of all that passed.

Blur of corn, stoic silos
green shadows in the forest

a young man who strode and
stretched across a prairie waving

and much later a lone firework
exploded raining its red light down

revealing what had been
hidden for so long for an instant.

Poems by Joe Guszkowski

What's Wrong?

The day begins without you

with wet hair bursting into the cafe.

Waiters balancing dirty plates

turn the other way.

Your friends meaning well

have messed up totally.

Instead of a party they gave you

twin boys hugging your ankles

calling you Three.

Magnetic dust

coats your thoughts.

Men and animals

come off the mountain

and pitch tents

in the waning light.

Tomorrow they expect

to make their presentation

and tonight you've barely

touched your eggs.

Face

We each have someone
secret to think about.

It’s snowing enough to count.

I’m looking in the window
and I don’t like what I see.

If you stop me, I won’t
explain it well, I’ll claw
at my face, you hate that.

Lake of Islands

Currants are funny
little fruits he said

with only his whole house
to lean on

grass fanning out
undiscerning

popping the sun
on a string.

Upstairs in the new place
she was morning sick

with all her friends
tied her in knots

and later at the picnic
she gave birth to them again

on a blanket spread
under the trees all

fussy like lovers
in a hammock.

Within that spring
there slept a dreadful summer.

The lake swallowed the path
so we turned back.
